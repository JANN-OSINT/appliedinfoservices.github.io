# Stop Slop — Defense & Intelligence Edition

Eliminate AI writing patterns from intelligence and defense analysis products without destroying the precision conventions that give those products their authority.

**This skill is not a plain-English converter.** ICD 203 probabilistic language, source-passive constructions, and institutional analytical voice are features, not bugs. The target is slop — performative openers, hollow structure, rhetorical emphasis that substitutes for evidence — not the professional conventions of finished intelligence.

---

## Core Rules

### 1. Cut Throat-Clearing Openers

Intelligence products begin with the assessment. Delete everything before the first substantive analytical claim.

**Kill on sight:**
- "In today's complex and rapidly evolving security environment..."
- "As geopolitical competition intensifies..."
- "Against a backdrop of unprecedented change..."
- "This report examines the complex dynamics of..."
- "It is important to understand that..."
- Any sentence whose deletion would not change what the reader learns

**Replace with:** The BLUF, the Key Judgment, or the first substantive claim.

---

### 2. Active Voice by Default; Passive Voice Only When Justified

Active voice is the default. Passive voice is permitted in three cases:

**Permitted passive:**
1. **Source protection** — "The facility was assessed as operational" (protects collection method or source)
2. **Unknown actor** — "The convoy was ambushed" (actor genuinely unknown at time of writing)
3. **ICD 203 standard language** — "It is assessed that..." / "It is estimated that..." (standard finished intelligence attribution)

**Ban false passivity** — passive voice used to avoid accountability or obscure the analyst's judgment:
- "A decision was made to..." → who decided?
- "It has been suggested that..." → by whom? Is this your assessment or a sourced claim?
- "Concerns have been raised..." → state the concern and attribute it
- "Mistakes were made..." → name who made them

---

### 3. Preserve ICD 203 Probabilistic Language; Kill Hedge Language

These are not interchangeable. One assigns calibrated confidence. The other avoids it.

**ICD 203 confidence qualifiers — PRESERVE ALL:**

| Term | Probability | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Almost certainly / nearly certain | >95% | High confidence assessments |
| Likely / probably | 55–95% | Moderate-high confidence |
| Roughly even chance / about as likely as not | 45–55% | Low confidence, genuine uncertainty |
| Unlikely / improbably | 20–45% | Moderate-low confidence |
| Remote / highly unlikely | <20% | Low probability scenarios |

Never delete ICD 203 qualifiers. Never replace "likely" with "may" or "could" — those do not convey the same confidence level. If the stated confidence level and supporting evidence appear mismatched, flag it — do not silently change it.

**Hedge language — DELETE:**
- "somewhat," "rather," "fairly," "quite," "relatively" (when not part of an ICD 203 qualifier)
- "it's worth noting that," "it should be mentioned that," "it bears emphasizing"
- "in many ways," "to a certain extent," "to some degree"
- Intensifiers used as filler: "clearly," "obviously," "undeniably"
- Softeners: "genuinely," "truly," "deeply," "fundamentally," "inherently"

**The test:** Does the qualifier assign a calibrated probability (ICD 203), or does it soften accountability? Preserve the former. Delete the latter.

---

### 4. Be Specific; No Vague Declaratives

Every analytical claim must be anchored to observable indicators, a timeframe, a named actor, or a specific mechanism.

**Vague (ban):**
- "Beijing is reportedly concerned about regional stability."
- "Russian forces have demonstrated significant capability improvements."
- "The situation remains fluid."
- "Tensions continue to rise."
- "The implications are significant."
- "The stakes are high."

**Specific (require):** Every claim should answer — what specifically, by how much, since when, according to what indicator?

Vague declaratives in intelligence products do not merely fail to inform — they can be mistaken for assessed findings.

---

### 5. Institutional Analytical Voice; No Vague Attribution

Third-person institutional voice is the standard for finished intelligence. Second-person ("you") is inappropriate in analytical products.

Do not use vague collective attribution:
- "Observers note..." → who specifically?
- "Analysts believe..." → which analysts? This analysis or cited external?
- "Some argue..." → attribute it or cut it
- "It has been widely reported..." → cite the reporting or drop the qualifier

**Instead:**
- State your assessment directly: "This analysis concludes..." / "The available indicators suggest..."
- Attribute competing assessments specifically: "DIA assessed in January 2026 that..."
- Own your Key Judgments: if it is your assessment, say so

Vague attribution launders analytical uncertainty as consensus and hides the analyst's confidence level.

---

### 6. Rhythm; No Em Dashes; No Dramatic Fragmentation

Defense/intelligence prose is measured and declarative. It does not perform urgency.

**Ban:**
- Em dashes (—) used for dramatic pause or amplification. Use commas or periods.
- Sentence fragments deployed for emphasis: "This is not a drill. Full stop."
- Staccato rhythm mimicking social media urgency
- Three-item lists used for rhetorical buildup — two items or one

**Binary contrast — conditional use:**
"This is not X. It is Y." is permitted when it performs genuine analytical disambiguation — distinguishing between two live competing interpretations where both are plausible to the reader. It is banned when it is purely rhetorical emphasis with no competing interpretation at stake.

- **Permitted:** "This is not opportunistic looting. It is systematic document collection — consistent with organized intelligence exploitation."
- **Banned:** "This is not a minor development. It is a turning point." (No analytical content in the contrast; pure rhetoric.)

---

### 7. Trust Analytical Authority; No Performative Emphasis

Intelligence products derive authority from evidence and methodology, not from emphasis devices.

**Ban:**
- "Let that sink in."
- "Read that again."
- "This cannot be overstated."
- "Full stop." / "Period."
- "Make no mistake."
- "Here's the thing:"
- Rhetorical questions used as setups: "What does this mean? Everything."
- "Perhaps most importantly..." / "Most critically..."
- "The uncomfortable truth is..."

These constructions transfer the work of convincing from evidence to performance. If the finding is significant, the evidence makes that case. If it does not, emphasis crutches do not fix it.

---

### 8. No Quotables; No Policy Prescription

**Ban abstract quotables:**
- "In an era defined by strategic competition, clarity of analysis is the ultimate force multiplier."
- "At its core, this is about who controls information."
- Mission statement language: "We are committed to delivering actionable intelligence to decision-makers at every level."

**Ban policy prescription masquerading as analysis:**
- "The United States must..."
- "Policymakers should prioritize..."
- "Washington needs to..."

Intelligence analysis describes and assesses. It does not prescribe. If the product includes policy implications, label them explicitly as implications and separate them from the assessment.

## Defense & Intelligence Jargon

Replace inflated or imprecise terminology:

| Replace | With | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| "actionable intelligence" | name the specific action it enables | "actionable" is filler if the action is unspecified |
| "kinetic effects" | "strikes," "fires," "attacks" | euphemism obscures the assessment |
| "contested environment" | specify what is contested and by whom | vague threat descriptor |
| "robust capability" | describe the specific capability | "robust" is an empty intensifier |
| "whole-of-government approach" | name the agencies and their roles | bureaucratic nominalization |
| "near-peer competitor" | name the competitor and the specific capability gap | hedge that avoids commitment |
| "escalatory potential" | "may escalate to" + specific next step | nominalizes a dynamic process |
| "threat vector" | describe the specific delivery method or attack path | technical-sounding but contentless |
| "multi-domain" | list the domains | empty compound adjective |
| "strategic ambiguity" | describe the specific ambiguous commitment | name what is ambiguous |
| "operationalize" | "implement," "execute," "apply" | corporate inflation |
| "deconflict" | "coordinate," "resolve conflict between" | jargon obscuring coordination failure |
| "posture" (verb) | "position," "deploy," "orient" | military jargon inflation |
| "game-changing capability" | describe the specific change to the operational calculus | "game-changing" quantifies nothing |
| "significant" (unmodified) | quantify the significance | empty intensifier |

## Structural Standards — Not Slop

These elements are product structure, not AI patterns. Do not remove them:

**Preserve:**
- **BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)** — required for finished intelligence
- **Key Judgments** — required for analytical products
- **Source attribution / confidence levels** — required for analytical integrity
- **Executive Summary** — appropriate for long-form products
- **Background / Context sections** — appropriate when the reader needs context to evaluate the assessment
- **Section headers** — intelligence products are structured documents, not essays

**The test:** Is this structure serving the reader's ability to navigate and evaluate the analysis, or is it substituting for analytical content? Preserve the former. Cut the latter.

---

## Quick Checks

Before delivering prose:

- **Opener:** Does the piece start with an assessment or a wind-up? Cut everything before the first substantive claim.
- **ICD 203 audit:** Identify every probabilistic qualifier. Verify each is the precise ICD 203 term for the intended confidence level. Flag mismatches — do not silently change.
- **Passive voice audit:** Flag every passive construction. Apply the three-test filter (source protection / unknown actor / ICD 203). Delete false passives.
- **Vague declarative scan:** Does every claim answer — what specifically, by how much, since when, according to what indicator? Flag claims that cannot.
- **Attribution audit:** Find every "observers note," "analysts believe," "some suggest." Replace with direct attribution or direct assertion.
- **Jargon scan:** Run the jargon table. Replace every hit.
- **Emphasis crutch scan:** Find "let that sink in," "full stop," "make no mistake," em dashes, fragments-for-profundity. Delete all.
- **Policy/prescription check:** Find every "must," "should," "needs to" directed at a named actor. Flag as prescription. Either cut or label explicitly as policy implication.
- **Adverb check:** Kill hedge adverbs. Preserve ICD 203 qualifiers.
- **Quotable check:** If it sounds like a pull quote or mission statement, rewrite it.

## Scoring

Rate 1–10 on each dimension. Minimum passing score: 42/60.

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| **Directness** | Does every sentence deliver analytical content? Is the assessment front-loaded? |
| **Precision** | Do all claims carry appropriate ICD 203 confidence levels, anchored to specific indicators and actors? |
| **Rhythm** | Is the prose measured and declarative — varied sentence length without performance? |
| **Analytical Authority** | Does the prose derive authority from evidence, not emphasis devices? Clean attribution on every Key Judgment? |
| **Specificity** | Are claims grounded in named actors, observable indicators, timeframes, and geographic specificity? |
| **Economy** | Is every sentence doing work? No wind-up, no structural announcements substituting for content? |

Below 42/60: revise.

## Examples

### Example 1 — Opener and Vague Declaratives

**Before:**
> In today's complex and rapidly evolving threat environment, understanding the dynamics of adversary behavior is more important than ever. This analysis examines recent developments in the South China Sea and their potential implications for U.S. Indo-Pacific strategy.

**After:**
> Chinese Coast Guard vessels conducted 14 water-cannon incidents against Philippine resupply vessels at Second Thomas Shoal between August 2023 and March 2026 — an almost certain indicator of a deliberate, sustained pressure campaign designed to normalize PRC maritime enforcement in the First Island Chain. This assessment examines the pattern, likely escalation trajectory, and implications for U.S. mutual defense commitments.

**Changes:** Throat-clearing opener deleted. "Potential implications" (vague) replaced with specific analytical frame. ICD 203 qualifier ("almost certainly") added to the pattern assessment. Specific actor, location, count, and timeframe substituted for generic framing.

---

### Example 2 — False Passive and Hedge Language

**Before:**
> Concerns have been raised about the facility's operational status. It has been suggested that production activities may have resumed, though this remains somewhat uncertain given the limited available information.

**After:**
> Commercial satellite imagery from 14 March 2026 shows resumed thermal signatures at the facility's eastern production building, consistent with prior operational patterns. We assess with moderate confidence that centrifuge assembly has likely resumed, based on increased vehicle activity and power draw indicators. Confidence is limited by the absence of human intelligence corroboration.

**Changes:** "Concerns have been raised" — false passive, no actor, deleted. "It has been suggested" — false attribution, replaced with direct assessment. "May have resumed" — replaced with ICD 203 "likely" with explicit confidence statement. "Somewhat uncertain" — hedge deleted, replaced with specific sourcing caveat. Passive in "satellite imagery shows" is appropriate — the collection is the subject.

---

### Example 3 — Emphasis Crutches and Quotables

**Before:**
> This is not merely a military development. It is a fundamental shift in the regional balance of power. Let that sink in. In an era defined by great power competition, the ability to project analytical clarity is the ultimate strategic advantage. Make no mistake — the decisions made in the next 90 days will shape the trajectory of the conflict for years to come.

**After:**
> PLA deployment of DF-17 hypersonic glide vehicles to the Eastern Theater Command represents a probable shift in China's first-strike targeting calculus against Taiwan. The DF-17's 1,800 km range and Mach 5+ terminal velocity almost certainly negates existing terminal-phase intercept capability, reducing warning time from approximately 12 minutes to under 4 minutes. Decisions made in the next 90 days regarding pre-delegation authority and dispersal posture will likely determine whether the U.S.-Taiwan defense relationship retains credible deterrent value.

**Changes:** "Let that sink in," "Make no mistake" — deleted. "Fundamental shift in the regional balance of power" — abstract quotable, replaced with specific capability change and its operational consequence. "An era defined by great power competition" — throat-clearing, deleted. Final sentence retained but grounded in a specific decision set with ICD 203 qualifier.

---

### Example 4 — Jargon and Vague Attribution

**Before:**
> Analysts believe that Iran is likely attempting to operationalize its proxy network to create a multi-domain, whole-of-government challenge to U.S. and partner forces across a contested environment. This represents a robust and kinetic capability with significant escalatory potential.

**After:**
> Iran is likely directing Hezbollah, the Houthis, and Iraqi Shia militia groups toward coordinated, sequenced operations across the Levant, Red Sea, and Iraq — almost certainly designed to force U.S. force dispersion and degrade coalition freedom of movement. The coordinating mechanism is the IRGC Quds Force; tactical execution authority likely remains with individual group commanders. The risk of escalation to direct Iran-U.S. exchange is unlikely but not remote, contingent on whether a Houthi strike kills U.S. military personnel.

**Changes:** "Analysts believe" — vague attribution deleted, assessment stated directly. "Operationalize" → "directing toward coordinated operations." "Multi-domain, whole-of-government challenge" → named actors and specific operating areas. "Contested environment" → described specifically. "Robust and kinetic capability" — empty, deleted. "Escalatory potential" → replaced with a specific escalation scenario and ICD 203 confidence level.
